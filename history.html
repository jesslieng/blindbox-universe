The Story Behind Blind Boxes

The concept traces back to Japan’s early 1900s “fukubukuro” mystery bags — sealed packages sold during New Year for surprise value. Later, in 1965, Ryugo Shigeta introduced Gachapon machines, offering small collectibles in clear plastic capsules.

These capsules weren't technically blind boxes because you could see the capsule, but they work like modern mystery boxes. Blind boxes have existed in many forms since the early days of gachapon, whether that be loot boxes in video games or booster packs of trading card games.
